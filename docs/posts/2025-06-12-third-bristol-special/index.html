Swing district special election results in a 24-vote margin for Democrat

Democrat Lisa Field wins Third Bristol special election to replace Carol Doherty by 24 votes

Brent Benson
2025-06-12

Figure 1: Third Bristol special election results

Swing districts dont swing every election, but the 24 vote margin in Tuesdays special election for Third Bristol State Representative emphasizes how close swing district elections can be, even when the winning party is the same as last election.

Lisa Field, the Democratic candidate from Taunton emerged with a 24 vote margin based on the results posted by Easton and Taunton election officials. Lawrence Quintal, the Republican candidate from Taunton, has said that he will request a recount which is not automatic, but requires a small number of signatures. Once the signatures have been certified, the state takes on the funding and organization of the recount. It is rare for a recount to change the result.

The special election took place because of the passing of Representative Carol Doherty (D-Taunton) who died in February from pancreatic cancer. Lisa Field is a lobbyist for the Massachusetts Nurses Association and Lawrence Quintal is the co-owner of Silva Funeral Home and a Taunton City Councilor.

Lisa Field won the Easton precincts by 72 votes, while Tauntons precincts created a 48 vote margin for Lawrence Quintal. Quintal had more votes than Field in seven precincts; Field had more votes in five precincts; and the candidates were tied with 50 votes each in Taunton Ward 2, Precinct A.

Fields margin of 0 points is about 3 points less than Harriss margin in November, 2024.

Based on the candidate fundraising reports to the Office of Campaign and Political Finance, both candidates raised over $50,000 and were able to put their case in front of prospective voters, resulting in a close election in a district that was represented by Republican Mayer Shaunna OConnell (R-Taunton) for almost ten years from 2011 until 2020 before Rep.Dohertys win in a 2020 special election.
